Characteristics

The fundamental modern architecture characteristics.

1

Open space plans

One of the hardest decorating challenges in open concept decorating is creating a cohesive design through the connected spaces. Whether your style is subtle or bold, patterns and textures add interest to your home and are an important part of your design.

2

Simple design

Simple design is an interior design style characterized by a monochromatic color palette, clean lines, minimalism, natural materials, and natural light.

3

Abundance of natural light

Embracing an abundance of natural light in your home is one of the best ways to bring the beauty of outdoors into one of the most personal rooms in your home, that will having you craving the glow of daylight.

Styles

Everything as simple as possible, but no simpler.

A stepped contour and sharp edges.

One of the earliest of the modern architecture styles, Art Deco is easy to spot. Structures designed in this style are characterized by vertical sections with a stepped contour and sharp edges consisting mainly of rectangular sections with curved shapes strategically placed for ornamentation.

The simplicity and utility rises.

Bauhaus, an inverted form of the German word for housebuilding, hausbau, was a school for architecture, design, and art. The Bauhaus style of architecture emphasizes functionality and the concept that the artistic appeal of a structure comes from the simplicity and utility of its design.
